Located in the crossover of the Eurasia, the Japanese archipelago, the Indo-China Peninsula and the Oceania, Taiwan has a dynamic evolving multicultural food history. In the past a few centries, cultual influx has molded the taste of Taiwan. Every cultural that arrived here took roots, bloomed and interweaved into the special Taiwanese food style.
Period Culture Influence
1975-Present International Community Brought in stronger International elements.
1945-1975 Migration from Mainland China 8 Chinese regional cuisines became another major basis of Taiwanese food.
1895-1945 Japanese Rice became staple. Cold, grilled and Teppanyaki kicked in.
1661-1895 Cantonese & Hakka Stir-fry and stew were most prevailing.
1624-1662 Indigenous Taro, sweet potato, boar meat and millet wine.
